What is Pair Programming?

Pair programming is the practice of collaborating on programming tasks / projects between 2 developers.
Collaborating in this way has been proven to be very effective both to accelerate the task / project and the learning process of the developers.
The following are the main types of pair programming:

  • Driver / Navigator
  • Ping Pong
  • Novice / Expert

Valerie's Reflection

This was actually my second time pairing, but my first time pairing with a guide. Overall, it was enjoyable and I learned a lot. I find pairing challenging because I am not used to thinking out loud. This is something I need to work on, maybe by practicing thinking through the challenges out loud when I'm by myself to get more used to it.

Because I had less experience with HTML and CSS, it seemed like the driver/navigator model broke down a bit, and by the end, I was being coached by Stefano. For me, this was great. I learned a lot, and he was encouraging me to think rather than dictating the answers. However, I'm worried that it was frustrating for him, and I also feel as though I didn't contribute equally to the session.

We did run into a few technical glitches with Google Hangout. When all three of us were on video and unmuted microphones, there was a lot of choppiness and feedback. We resolved this issue by turning off the video after the check-in (we turned it back on at the end to say goodbye), and our guide turned off his microphone unless he wanted to speak. That worked out well.

Stefano's Reflection

I am so very happy that we went through this pairing session. Pairing is always a great opportunity to learn both at a technical level and at a human level, both of which are very crucial aspects of a developer's success.

Technically it was good to be reminded to stay focused and learn how to catch the tiniest details (another very important skill for a developer).
At a human level this was a very enriching experience in terms of learning how to be of best support and realize once again that productivity is not goal. It is how we grow together as a team, how we build together that matters most, and from experience I can say that once we know that this is the focus, productivity will naturally be great.

I am grateful to have more and more opportunities to grow and learn from others.
